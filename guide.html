Guidance for your Better Security!

PHISHING

Phishing is when attackers impersonate trusted companies, banks, or services to trick users into revealing passwords, financial information, or personal data. They use fake emails, messages, or websites that look convincingly real. More targeted versions like spear-phishing focus on individuals using personal details, while smishing uses SMS instead of email

PASSWORD ATTACKS

Password attacks exploit weak, short, or reused passwords. Attackers use techniques like brute force (trying every combination), dictionary attacks (trying common words), and credential-stuffing (using leaked passwords from other sites). If a password is predictable or reused, attackers can often break in without much effort.

MALWARE

Malware is harmful software that infects devices through downloads, email attachments, fake apps, or compromised websites. Variants include ransomware that locks your files, spyware that secretly monitors you, trojans disguised as harmless programs, and worms that spread automatically. Most infections happen because users trust unsafe files or links.

UNSAFE BROWSING

Unsafe browsing exposes users to malicious websites, dangerous pop-ups, and tracking scripts. Accessing sensitive accounts over public Wi-Fi can lead to man-in-the-middle attacks where hackers intercept your data. Visiting unsecured HTTP websites or clicking suspicious ads can expose your device to viruses or data theft.

HOW TO STAY SAFE

Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi‑factor authentication on all important accounts. Never click links or download attachments from unexpected messages, even if they look official. Stick to HTTPS websites, avoid entering sensitive information on public Wi‑Fi, and only download apps or software from trusted sources. Keep your browser and devices updated, block third‑party trackers, and rely on a reputable antivirus tool. Most attacks succeed because of small careless decisions — fix those, and you eliminate most risks.
